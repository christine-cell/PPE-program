Section 01 · The Basics

What Are Supplements?

Dietary supplements are substances used to add nutrients to your diet or help address certain health concerns. These concerns may include digestive problems, osteoporosis, arthritis, or general nutrient deficiencies.

If you're looking to support your health, getting the right amount of vitamins and minerals each day is essential. When used appropriately, supplements may help support energy levels, mood, mental clarity, and overall well-being.

⚠️ Supplements should never replace a healthy diet. If you are eating a whole-foods–based diet — focusing on nutrient-dense foods and limiting inflammatory foods and excess dairy — you are already one big step ahead.

A healthy diet supports your gut, and a healthy gut plays an important role in supporting your immune system. Supplements work best as an addition to good nutrition, not a substitute for it.

Section 02 · Gut Health

Gut Health & Probiotics

One of the first places I like to start when discussing supplements is gut health — and that often brings us to probiotics.

Probiotics are beneficial bacteria that can help support a healthy balance in your digestive system. They may be helpful for people experiencing digestive concerns such as indigestion, bloating, or constipation.

Our gut naturally contains a mix of both good and bad bacteria, and probiotics help replenish the beneficial bacteria that keep our digestive system functioning properly.

A high-quality probiotic supplement can help support the restoration of that balance.

For example, if you have ever:

💉
Taken antibiotics — these can wipe out beneficial bacteria along with harmful ones
🤢
Experienced a stomach virus — illness can throw off your gut's natural balance
🦠
Had a parasite or significant digestive illness — these situations can disrupt the balance of bacteria in your gut

💡 Many people do not need to stay on probiotics indefinitely. Instead, they are often used for a period of time to help improve gut health, depending on your individual needs.

Section 03 · Food as Medicine

Foods That Support Gut Health

In addition to supplements, certain foods can also help support beneficial bacteria in the gut.

🍖
Bone Broth
Ideally grass-fed and organic. Rich in collagen and gut-healing compounds.
🌶️
Kimchi
Fermented vegetables packed with beneficial bacteria.
🥬
Sauerkraut
Fermented cabbage — a classic probiotic-rich food.
🥛
Kefir
Fermented milk drink — not ideal for everyone. Introduce gradually.
⚠️
A Word of Caution on Fermented Foods

Fermented foods are not right for everyone. If you have underlying digestive issues, they can sometimes worsen symptoms. That's why it's important to introduce these foods gradually and ideally under the guidance of a healthcare professional. Understanding the root cause of digestive symptoms is always the first step.
